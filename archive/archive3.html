February 2010: Educational uses for WIKIs

Description: What can I do with a wiki? Wikis, collaboratively edited websites, let any visitor become a participant. You can edit the actual content without any technical or programming knowledge; all you need is a computer with an Internet connection. Wikis can be used to collaborate on projects, to create lesson summaries, for e-Portfolios, to synthesize research and present information, and for meeting support – just to name a few ideas.

Join us for a webinar on the uses of wikis in teaching and learning and see how you can transform your classroom into an interactive, collaborative learning space.

Presenter(s): Dr. Nancy Rubin began her career in eLearning in 2000 as an Instructional Designer at a state university in Florida and had responsibilities for the oversight of a large-scale Blackboard implementation, instructional designers, and the faculty and staff training department. More recently, Dr. Rubin was a Senior Level Instructional Designer and then the Curriculum Specialist, for a large for-profit online university. As the curriculum specialist, Dr. Rubin reviewed course curriculum and identified opportunities to integrate web 2.0 tools and literacies into assignments.

March 2010: Grading Papers the Electronic Way

Description: Have you ever lost a hard-copy of a student’s work? Are you getting tired of carrying around stacks of paper? Have you ever considered how you could use less paper in your course? If you answered yes to these questions, this webinar is for you! In this webinar you will be introduced to some of the advanced features of Microsoft Word that can assist you with adding constructive feedback to your students' electronic submissions. In addition to learning about the value of meaningful feedback, you will also be introduced to such tools as Comments, Track Changes and using Macros.

Presenter(s): Sue Schulte & Nancy Epner. Sue and Nancy have spent more than 10 years in Humber's Instructional Support Studio helping faculty use technology more effectively in teaching and learning.

April 2010: Structuring Online Discussions

Description: On-line discussion forums meet the needs of the 24/7 generation by extending the teaching and learning environment beyond the confines of the traditional classroom. How does one structure these experiences to maximize learning and yet keep the workload manageable for both you and your learners?

During this session we will explore key concepts for implementing online discussions as one element of instruction, tips for structuring and grading online discussions, and share experiences in using online discussion forums.

Presenter(s): Pat Anderchek, Professor/Instructional Designer (CCW, BA/BSW, GDDE) Pat is a Professor at Mohawk College, presently on sabbatical, completing her Masters in Distance Education through Athabasca University. Over the last 10 years, while as the Coordinator of the Child and Youth Worker Program and then in her role as the Instructional Strategist in eLearning Services, she worked with faculty across several campuses in the design and delivery of their online and blended learning spaces. Pat has developed faculty educational programs with a focus on the advancement of pedagogical outcomes through the integration of learning technologies. She has experiences with online discussion forums from the perspective of both the educator and as a learner.

May 2010: Getting Ready for Virtual Learning Spaces

Description: Whether you are teaching a web-enhanced, hybrid or fully online course, Virtual Learning Spaces are impacting the way students collaborate and faculty deliver and share their knowledge.

Attend this session and learn about the features of Wimba Classroom (soon to be available through Humber’s Blackboard Learning Management System). This webinar will focus on setting rules of engagement, preparing your students to actively participate in the environment and other best practices you can build into your teaching.

Presenter(s): Jennifer Nowotny is a Senior Learning Manager at Wimba and has been with the company for almost five years. Prior to her employment at Wimba, she worked as an Instructional Technologist in the Teaching and Learning Program at California State University, Chico where she helped support the distance education program. During this session, she will share best practices and tips for using Wimba Classroom in the live teaching environment.

June 2010: Bring in a Guest Speaker - Virtually!

Description: Do you have a colleague or contact that is willing to speak to your class but scheduling is complicated and travel costs are too high? Consider making the connection using Wimba.

Attend this session to witness firsthand what it is like to be a student participating in a virtual lecture. During this session we will show you how you can bring speakers into your class using the technology presently available in Humber's eClassrooms.
