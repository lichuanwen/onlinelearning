Session 2b01
Workshop Title: Weight loss: Why is it so difficult to keep it off?
Presenters: Sergiu Fediuc

Description: One of the most common health related goals for many individuals living in western societies is weight loss. Manipulating the diet by restricting caloric intake and increasing caloric expenditure through exercise has traditionally been the most successful method of achieving this goal. However, once the target weight is reached, keeping the weight off permanently is a big challenge that most individuals fail at. This lecture will discuss how the human body adapts to weight loss, common misconceptions about metabolic regulation during times of caloric restriction, and the role of exercise in maintaining a healthy weight.

Sergiu Fediuc completed his Master's degree in Exercise Physiology and Stress research in 2006 and his Ph.D. in Human Metabolism in 2009. Both degrees were obtained from York University. Currently, Sergiu teaches in the Fitness and Health Promotion and Kinesiology departments at Humber College and the University of Guelph-Humber, respectively.

Session 306
Workshop Title: Finding Donna Lee
Presenter: Andrew Scott, Pat Labarbera

Description: Contrafacts--compositions whose melody and lyrics are original but whose form and harmony are borrowed--historically provided a way for musicians to record familiar music, while sidestepping issues of intellectual appropriation.

Famous among contrafacts is "Donna Lee," a line based upon "Indiana." The authorship of "Donna Lee" is contested with credit attributed to Charlie Parker and, more commonly, Miles Davis. Absent, however, is any mention of Tiny Kahn's "Tiny's Con," a 1946 composition that evidences striking similarities to the line Davis recorded (and was awarded authorship of) one year later.

Through transcription, interview, analysis and demonstration, we uncover a jazz mystery.

Andrew Scott is a Professor of Music at Humber where he oversees the Music Business and Music Marketing areas and works as Head academic advisor. He is a guitarist.

Pat Labarbera is a Professor of Music at Humber where he teaches private lessons, an ensemble and courses in jazz repertoire development, advanced jazz theory and performance practice. He is among the most accomplished jazz saxophonists in the world.
